Projects (Under Construction)

While many of the projects I’ve worked on are confidential or proprietary to the company, I can share a few that I’m particularly proud of and that reflect my skills and contributions.

Programming Pi

During a period when I was deeply exploring microcontrollers, I created this video to demonstrate how to properly flash and upload retro console game emulators onto a Raspberry Pi 3. It was an exciting project that introduced me to SSH, remote file transfer, and the process of loading an operating system onto a microcontroller—laying the groundwork for my interest in embedded systems.

Repairing Board

My curiosity was sparked when the master window switch in my dad’s car—responsible for controlling all the windows—stopped working. I took the opportunity to investigate the failure, and in the process, I learned how to use a digital multimeter (DMM) and perform detailed visual inspections to diagnose and repair faulty electrical components.

Educational Videos

While in college, I often found that certain engineering concepts weren’t given enough time or depth during lectures. I used these moments as an opportunity to apply what I had learned over the years to create more efficient ways of approaching and solving engineering problems. To this day, I continue to upload videos that I believe can help other aspiring engineers better understand complex topics and improve their problem-solving skills.

Arduino & RGB

I really enjoyed learning how to program Arduinos to create dynamic effects with programmable RGB LEDs. This was one of my first hands-on projects using both Arduino and RGB lighting, and it sparked my interest in embedded systems and creative electronics.

Battery Replacement

This video covers basic fundamentals of electrical engineering, where I explain the differences between series and parallel battery configurations, and demonstrate how to diagnose and replace faulty batteries on an electric moped.

Webpage Development
